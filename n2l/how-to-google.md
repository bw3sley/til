# How to Google
This is an article that will cover some ways to improve your search method.

## Removing words from a search
Put `-` before a word to remove it from the results.
    
Eg: `how to get an input value -jquery` .

## Searching by an exact expression    
Put a word or phrase inside quotation marks to find only results that are exactly to the expression.
    
Eg: `cannot read property 'map' of undefined` .

## Combining searches
Put `OR` or `|` between each expression that you want to search to combine them.
    
Eg: `marathon OR race` or `(marathon | race) sprint` .    

## Searching by specific site
Put `site:` before an URL to find only results from a specific website.

Eg: `site:fireship.io react hooks` .

## Searching by related results
Put `related:` before an URL to find results that are related to.

Eg: `related:time.com` .

## Seeing Google cache version from a site
Put `cache:` before an URL to see the Google cache version from a site.

Eg: `cache:www.rocketseat.com.br` .

## Searching after or before a specific date
Put `after` or `before:` in a phrase to find by results that are newer/older to.

Eg: `react hooks after:2021` or `react hooks before:2021` .

## Searching by a period of time
Put `..` between two words to find only values that were posted between that period.
    
Eg: `angular 2 announcement 2013..2015` .

## Searching by a file type
Put `:filetype` after a word or phrase to find files that refer to your search.
    
Eg: `clean code:PDF`.

## Searching using wildcards
Put `*` operator between a word or phrase to find everything related to your search.

Eg: `site:*fireship.io -www` or `how to design * with tailwindcss` .
